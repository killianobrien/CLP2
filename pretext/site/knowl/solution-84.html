Solution 1.3.2.17.1
\begin{align*} &\diff{}{x}\left\{e^x\left(x^3-3x^2+6x-6\right)\right\}\\ &=e^x\left(3x^2-6x+6\right)+e^x\left(x^3-3x^2+6x-6\right) \quad\mbox{(product rule)}\\ &=e^x\left(3x^2-6x+6+x^3-3x^2+6x-6\right)\\ &=x^3e^x\\ \end{align*}

So,

\begin{align*} \int x^3e^x\dee{x}&=e^x\left(x^3-3x^2+6x-6\right)+C \end{align*}
