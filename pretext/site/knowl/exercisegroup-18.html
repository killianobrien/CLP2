17

The shape of the earth is often approximated by an oblate spheroid, rather than a sphere. An oblate spheroid is formed by rotating an ellipse about its minor axis (its shortest diameter).

  1. Find the volume of the oblate spheroid obtained by rotating the upper (positive) half of the ellipse \((ax)^2+(by)^2=1\) about the \(x\)-axis, where \(a\) and \(b\) are positive constants with \(a \geq b\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose  12  the earth has radius at the equator of 6378.137 km, and radius at the poles of 6356.752 km. If we model the earth as an oblate spheroid formed by rotating the upper half of the ellipse \((ax)^2+(by)^2=1\) about the \(x\)-axis, what are \(a\) and \(b\text{?}\)
  3. What is the volume of this model of the earth? (Use a calculator.)
  4. Suppose we had calculated the volume of the earth by modelling it as a sphere with radius \(6378.137\) km. What would our absolute and relative errors be, compared to our oblate spheroid calculation?
18 (✳)

Let \(R\) be the bounded region that lies between the curve \(y = 4 - (x - 1)^2\) and the line \(y = x + 1\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch \(R\) and find its area.
  2. Write down a definite integral giving the volume of the region obtained by rotating \(R\) about the line \(y = 5\text{.}\) Do not evaluate this integral.
19 (✳)

Let \(\cR=\big\{(x,y)\ :\ (x-1)^2+y^2\le 1\text{ and } x^2+(y-1)^2\le 1\ \big\}\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch \(\cR\) and find its area.
  2. If \(\cR\) rotates around the \(y\)--axis, what volume is generated?
20 (✳)

Let \(\cR\) be the plane region bounded by \(x=0,\ x=1,\ y=0\) and \(y=c\sqrt{1+x^2}\text{,}\) where \(c\ge 0\) is a constant.

  1. Find the volume \(V_1\) of the solid obtained by revolving \(\cR\) about the \(x\)--axis.
  2. Find the volume \(V_2\) of the solid obtained by revolving \(\cR\) about the \(y\)--axis.
  3. If \(V_1=V_2\text{,}\) what is the value of \(c\text{?}\)

22

On a particular, highly homogeneous  13  planet, we observe that the density of the atmosphere \(h\) kilometres above the surface is given by the equation \(\rho(h) = c2^{-h/6}\quad \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m^3}}\text{,}\) where \(c\) is the density on the planet's surface.

  1. What is the mass of the atmosphere contained in a vertical column with radius one metre, sixty kilometres high?
  2. What height should a column be to contain \(\dfrac{3000c\pi}{\log 2}\) kilograms of air?
