Item
To test the convergence of the integral, we make the substitution \(u=\log x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=\frac{\dee{x}}{x}\text{.}\)
\begin{gather*} \int_2^R \frac{\dee{x}}{x (\log x)^p} =\int_{\log 2}^{\log R}\frac{\dee{u}}{u^p} \end{gather*}
We already know that the integral \(\int_1^\infty\frac{\dee{u}}{u^p}\text{,}\) and hence the integral \(\int_2^R \frac{\dee{x}}{x (\log x)^p}\text{,}\) converges if and only if \(p \gt 1\text{.}\)
