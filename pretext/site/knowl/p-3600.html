The question tells you \(\frac{1}{1-0}\int_0^1 f(x)\,\dee{x} = \frac{f(0)+f(1)}{2}\text{.}\) Note \(f(0)=c\text{,}\) and \(f(1) = a+b+c\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{1-0}\int_0^1 f(x)\,\dee{x} &= \frac{f(0)+f(1)}{2} = \frac{c+(a+b+c)}{2}\\ \int_0^1 \big(ax^2+bx+c \big)\,\dee{x}&=\frac{a+b+2c}{2}\\ \left[\frac{a}{3}x^3+\frac{b}{2}x^2+cx \right]_0^1&=\frac{a}{2}+\frac{b}{2}+c\\ \frac{a}{3}+\frac{b}{2}+c&=\frac{a}{2}+\frac{b}{2}+c\\ \frac{a}{3}&=a\\ a&=0 \end{align*}

That is, \(f(x)\) is linear.
