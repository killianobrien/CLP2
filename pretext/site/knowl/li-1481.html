Solution 2: First substitute \(y=1+x^2\text{,}\) \(\dee{y}=2x\,\dee{x}\text{.}\)
\begin{gather*} \int_0^1 x\log(1+x^2)\,\dee{x} =\frac{1}{2}\int_1^2\log y\ \dee{y} \end{gather*}
Then integrate by parts, using \(u=\log y\) and \(\dee{v}=\dee{y}\text{,}\) so that \(\dee{u}=\frac{1}{y}\text{,}\) \(v=y\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \int_0^1 x\log(1+x^2)\,\dee{x} \amp=\frac{1}{2}\int_1^2\log y\ \dee{y}\\ \amp=\Big[\frac{1}{2} y\log y\Big]_1^2-\frac{1}{2}\int_1^2 y\frac{1}{y}\,\dee{y} =\log 2-\frac{1}{2}\\ \amp\approx 0.193 \end{align*}
