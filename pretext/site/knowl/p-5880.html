  • \(E\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)=\dfrac{-5^7}{14\cdot 6^7}\left[\left(\frac12 \right)^{-7}+\left(\frac32 \right)^{-7} \right]=\dfrac{-5^7}{14\cdot 6^7}\left[2^7+\left(\frac23 \right)^{7} \right]\)

We want to decide which of these numbers is biggest, and which smallest. Note that \(2^7\) is much, much bigger than \((3/2)^7\text{,}\) and both \((3/4)^7\) and \((2/3)^7\) are less than one. Furthermore, \((3/2)^7\) is much larger than 2. So: \(\left[2^7+(2/3)^7\right] \gt \left[(3/2)^7+(3/4)^7\right] \gt 2.\) Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{-5^7}{14\cdot 6^7}\left[2^7+\left(\frac23 \right)^{7} \right] \lt \dfrac{-5^7}{14\cdot 6^7}\left[\left(\frac34 \right)^{7}+\left(\frac32 \right)^{7} \right] \lt \dfrac{-5^7}{14\cdot 6^7}[2] \end{equation*}
