Recall, from Theorem 3.6.7, that, for all \(-1\lt x\le 1\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \log(1+x) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty(-1)^k\frac{x^{k+1}}{k+1} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^{n-1}\frac{x^n}{n} \end{equation*}

(To get from the first sum to the second sum we substituted \(n=k+1\text{.}\) If you don't see why the two sums are equal, write out the first few terms of each.) So

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n\, 2^n} =\Big[\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^{n-1}\frac{x^n}{n}\Big]_{x=1/2} =\Big[\log(1+x)\Big]_{x=1/2} =\log(3/2) \end{gather*}
