Item 1.9.2.28.a
Suppose we are evaluating an integral that contains the term \(\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\text{,}\) where \(a\) is a positive constant, and we use the substitution \(x=a\sin u\) (with inverse \(u = \arcsin(x/a)\)), so that
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{a^2-x^2} = \sqrt{a^2\cos^2u}= |a\cos u| \end{equation*}
Under what circumstances is \(|a\cos u|\neq a\cos u\text{?}\)
