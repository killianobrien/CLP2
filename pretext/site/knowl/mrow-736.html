\begin{align*} \int 9\sin^8(x) \cos(x) \dee{x} &= \int 9u(x)^8 \cdot u'(x) \dee{x}\\ &= \int 9u^8 \dee{u} \bigg|_{u=\sin(x)} & \text{by the substitution rule}\\ \end{align*}

We are now left with the problem of antidifferentiating a monomial; this we can do with Theorem 1.3.17.

\begin{align*} &= \left( u^9 +C \right)\bigg|_{u=\sin(x)}\\ &= \sin^9(x) +C \end{align*}
