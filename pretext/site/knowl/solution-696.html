Solution 3.6.8.12.1

Since

\begin{equation*} f'(t) =\diff{}{t}\log(1+2t) = \frac{2}{1+2t} = 2\sum_{n=0}^\infty(-2t)^n \quad\text{if }|2t| \lt 1\text{ i.e. }|t| \lt \frac{1}{2} \end{equation*}

and \(f(0)=0\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \amp f(x) =\int_0^x f'(t) \,\dee{t} =2 \sum_{n=0}^\infty \int_0^x (-1)^n 2^n t^n\,\dee{t} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n 2^{n+1} \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}\\ \amp\text{ for all }|x| \lt \frac{1}{2}. \end{align*}
