First, we differentiate.

\begin{align*} f(x)&=\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}A_n(x-c)^n\\ f'(x)&=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}nA_n(x-c)^{n-1}\\ &=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}nA_n(x-c)^{n-1}\\ f'(c)&=\sum_{n=1}^\infty nA_n\cdot 0^{n-1}\\ &=A_1\cdot 1 + 2A_2\cdot0+3A_3\cdot0+\cdots\\ &=A_1 \end{align*}

So, if \(A_1=0\text{,}\) then \(f'(c)=0\text{.}\) That is, \(f(x)\) has a critical point at \(x=c\text{.}\)
