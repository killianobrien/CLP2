Solution 3.6.8.11.1

We found the Taylor series for \(e^{3x}\) from scratch in QuestionÂ 8. If we hadn't just done that, we could easily find it by modifying the series for \(e^x\text{.}\)

Substituting \(y=3x\) into the exponential series

\begin{equation*} e^y=\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{y^n}{n!} \end{equation*}

gives

\begin{equation*} e^{3x}=\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(3x)^n}{n!} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{3^n\,x^n}{n!} \end{equation*}

so that \(c_5\text{,}\) the coefficient of \(x^5\text{,}\) which appears only in the \(n=5\) term, is \(c_5=\dfrac{3^5}{5!}\)
