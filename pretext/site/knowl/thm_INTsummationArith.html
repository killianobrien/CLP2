Theorem 1.1.5 Arithmetic of Summation Notation

Let \(n\ge m\) be integers. Then for all real numbers \(c\) and \(a_i,b_i\text{,}\) \(m\le i\le n\text{.}\)

  1. \(\sum\limits_{i=m}^nca_i = c\bigg(\sum\limits_{i=m}^na_i\bigg)\)
  2. \(\sum\limits_{i=m}^n(a_i+b_i) = \bigg(\sum\limits_{i=m}^na_i\bigg) + \bigg(\sum\limits_{i=m}^nb_i\bigg)\)
  3. \(\sum\limits_{i=m}^n(a_i-b_i) = \bigg(\sum\limits_{i=m}^na_i\bigg) - \bigg(\sum\limits_{i=m}^nb_i\bigg)\)
