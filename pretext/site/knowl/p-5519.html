This allows us to evaluate our series.

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{n}{2^n}-\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{n}{3^n} &=\frac{\frac12}{\left(1-\frac12\right)^2}-\frac{\frac13}{\left(1-\frac13\right)^2}\\ &=2-\frac{3}{4} = \frac{5}{4}\\ \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} \bar x &=\frac{5/4}{3/2}=\frac{5}{6} = 0.8\overline{33} \end{align*}

Remark: we can check that this makes some sense. Since the weights to the right of \(x=0\) are heavier than those to the left, but spaced the same, we would expect our rod to balance to the right of \(x=0\text{.}\)
