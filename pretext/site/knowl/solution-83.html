Solution 1.3.2.16.1
\begin{align*} \diff{}{x}\{x\log(ax)-x\}&=x\left(\frac{a}{ax}\right)+\log(ax)-1\quad\mbox{(product rule, chain rule)}\\ &=\log(ax)\\ \end{align*}

So, we know

\begin{align*} \int \log(ax)\dee{x}&= x\log(ax)-x+C \end{align*}

where \(a\) is a given constant, and \(C\) is any constant.
