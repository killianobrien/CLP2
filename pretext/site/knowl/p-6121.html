Once you have found a root \(r\) of a polynomial, even if you cannot factor \((x-r)\) out of the polynomial by inspection, you can find \(Q(x)\) by dividing \(P(x)\) by \(x-r\text{,}\) using the long division algorithm you learned  7  in school, but with \(10\) replaced by \(x\text{.}\)
