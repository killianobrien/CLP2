(b) The power of cosine is odd, so we can reserve one cosine for the differential and change the rest to sines. Substituting \(y=\sin x,\) \(\dee{y}=\cos x\text{,}\) \(\dee{x},\) \(y(0)=0,\) \(y(\pi/2)=1,\) \(\cos^2x=1-y^2\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int_0^{\pi/2}\hskip-.2in\cos^3x\ \sin^2x\,\dee{x} &=\int_0^{\pi/2}\hskip-.2in\cos^2x\ \sin^2x\ \cos x\,\dee{x}\\ \amp=\int_0^1\!(1-y^2)y^2\,\dee{y} =\int_0^1(y^2-y^4)\,\dee{y}\\ &=\bigg[\frac{y^3}{3}-\frac{y^5}{5}\bigg]_0^1 =\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{5}\\ &= \frac{2}{15} \end{align*}
