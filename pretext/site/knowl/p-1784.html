(a) Using the trig identity \(\tan^2x=\sec^2 x-1\) and the substitution \(y=\tan x\text{,}\) \(\dee{y}=\sec^2 x\ \dee{x}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int\tan^nx\dee{x} &=\int\tan^{n-2}x\ \tan^2x\dee{x}\\ &=\int\tan^{n-2}x\ \sec^2x\dee{x}-\int\tan^{n-2}x\dee{x}\\ &=\int y^{n-2}\,\dee{y}-\int\tan^{n-2}x\dee{x}\\ &=\frac{y^{n-1}}{n-1}-\int\tan^{n-2}x\dee{x}\\ &=\frac{\tan^{n-1}x}{n-1} -\int\tan^{n-2}x\dee{x} \end{align*}
