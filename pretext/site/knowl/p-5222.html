Using the fact that our language's 20,000 words make up 100% of the words used, we can find a lower bound for \(\alpha\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 100&= \sum_{n=1}^{20,000} \frac{\alpha}{n} \gt \int_1^{20,001} \frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x} =\Big[\alpha \log(x)\Big]_{1}^{20,001} =\alpha\log(20,001)\\ \alpha& \lt \frac{100}{\log(20,001)} \end{align*}

We can find an upper bound for \(\alpha\) in a similar manner.
