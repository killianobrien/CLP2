Item f
The ODE (B.2.1) is said to be homogeneous if \(F(t)\) is identically zero. Otherwise, it is said to be inhomogeneous or nonhomogeneous. For example, the ODE \(y''(t)+7y(t)=0\) is homogeneous, while \(y''(t)+7y(t)=\sin t\) is inhomogeneous. A homogeneous ODE always has the trivial solution \(y(t)=0\text{.}\)
