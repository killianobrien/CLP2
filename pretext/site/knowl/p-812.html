Apply \(\diff{}{x}\) to both sides of \(x \sin(\pi x) = \int_0^x f(t)\, \dee{t}\text{.}\) Then, by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 1,

\begin{alignat*}{4} &&f(x) =\diff{}{x} \int_0^x f(t)\, \dee{t}&=\diff{}{x}\big\{x \sin(\pi x)\big\}\\ \implies&& f(x) &= \diff{}{x}\big\{x \sin(\pi x)\big\} =\sin(\pi x)+\pi x\cos(\pi x) \\ \implies&& f(4)&= \sin(4\pi)+4\pi\cos(4\pi)=4\pi \end{alignat*}
