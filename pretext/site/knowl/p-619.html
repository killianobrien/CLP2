The symbol

\begin{gather*} \left.\int f(x)\dee{x} \right|_{a}^{b} \end{gather*}

denotes the change in an antiderivative of \(f(x)\) from \(x=a\) to \(x=b\text{.}\) More precisely, let \(F(x)\) be any antiderivative of \(f(x)\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \left.\int f(x)\dee{x} \right|_{a}^{b} &= \left.F(x)\right|_a^b = F(b) - F(a) \end{align*}
