\begin{align*} e^y\,\dee{y} &= 2x\,\dee{x}.\\ \end{align*}

Integrating both sides:

\begin{align*} \int e^y\,\dee{y} &= \int 2x\,\dee{x}\\ e^y &= x^2+C\\ \end{align*}

Since \(y=\log2\) when \(x=0\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} e^{\log 2} &= 0^2+C\\ 2 &= C,\\ \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} e^{y} &= x^2+2\\ y &= \log(x^2+2) \end{align*}
