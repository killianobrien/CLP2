\begin{align*} \frac{1}{e-1}\int_1^e x^2\log x\dee{x} &\\ \end{align*}

To antidifferentiate, we use integration by parts with \(u=\log x\) and \(\dee{v}=x^2\,\dee{x}\text{,}\) hence \(\dee{u}=\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}\) and \(v=\frac{1}{3}x^3\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{e-1}\int_1^e x^2\log x\dee{x}&= \frac{1}{e-1}\left(\left[\frac{1}{3}x^3\log x\right]_1^e - \int_1^e \frac{1}{3}x^2\,\dee{x} \right)\\ &= \frac{1}{e-1}\bigg[\frac{x^3}{3}\log x-\frac{x^3}{9}\bigg]_{x=1}^{x=e}\\ &=\frac{1}{e-1}\bigg[\frac{e^3}{3}-\frac{e^3}{9}+\frac{1}{9}\bigg]\\ &=\frac{1}{e-1}\bigg[\frac{2}{9}e^3+\frac{1}{9}\bigg] \end{align*}
