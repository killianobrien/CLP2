Alternatively, the cable has linear density \(8\) kg\({}/5\) m\({}= 1.6\) kg\({}/{}\)m, and so the work required to lift a small piece of the cable (of length \(\Delta y\)) from height \(y\) m to height \(5\) m is

\begin{equation*} \underbrace{\underbrace{1.6\Delta y}_{\text{mass}}\cdot\underbrace{9.8\vphantom{y}}_{\text{gravity}}}_{\text{force}}\cdot\underbrace{(5-y)}_{\text{distance}}. \end{equation*}

The total work required is therefore

\begin{align*} \int_0^{5} 1.6\cdot9.8(5-y) \,\dee{y} \amp= 1.6\cdot9.8\left[5y-\frac{1}{2}y^2\right]_0^5\\ \amp=1.6\cdot9.8\cdot\left(25-\frac{25}{2}\right) = 196\ \mathrm{J} \end{align*}
