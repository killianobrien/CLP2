(b) The partial sum

\begin{align*} \amp\sum_{k=1}^N\Big(\frac{a_k}{a_{k+1}} -\frac{a_{k+1}}{a_{k+2}}\Big)\\ &\hskip0.5in =\Big(\frac{a_1}{a_2}-\frac{a_2}{a_3}\Big) +\Big(\frac{a_2}{a_3}-\frac{a_3}{a_4}\Big) +\cdots+ \Big(\frac{a_N}{a_{N+1}}-\frac{a_{N+1}}{a_{N+2}}\Big)\\ &\hskip0.5in =\frac{a_1}{a_2}-\frac{a_{N+1}}{a_{N+2}} \end{align*}

We are told that \({\displaystyle \sum_{k=1}^\infty \Big(\frac{a_k}{a_{k+1}} -\frac{a_{k+1}}{a_{k+2}}\Big) =\frac{a_1}{a_2} }\text{.}\) This means that the above partial sum converges to \(\frac{a_1}{a_2}\) as \(N\rightarrow\infty\text{,}\) or equivalently, that

\begin{gather*} \lim_{N\rightarrow\infty}\frac{a_{N+1}}{a_{N+2}}=0 \end{gather*}

and hence that

\begin{gather*} \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{|a_{k+1}(x-1)^{k+1}|}{|a_k(x-1)^k|} =|x-1|\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{|a_{k+1}|}{|a_k|} \end{gather*}

is infinite for any \(x\ne 1\text{.}\) So, by the ratio test, this series converges only for \(x=1\text{.}\)
