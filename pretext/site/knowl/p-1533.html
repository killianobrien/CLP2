Option 1 seems preferable. We integrate by parts, using \(u = x\text{,}\) \(\dee{v} =\cos x \,\dee{x}\) so that \(v=\sin x\) and \(\dee{u} = \dee{x}\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} x\cos x\,\dee{x} &= \underbrace{x}_u\underbrace{\sin x}_v \Big|_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} -\int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \underbrace{\sin x}_v\,\underbrace{\dee{x}}_{\dee{u}}\\ &= \Big[x\sin x +\cos x\Big]_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}} =\frac{\pi}{2} - 1 \end{align*}
