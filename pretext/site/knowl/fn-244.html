Footnote B.2.6
The reason that \(y(t)=e^{rt}\) is a good guess is that, with this guess, all of \(y(t)\text{,}\) \(y'(t)\) and \(y''(t)\) are constants times \(e^{rt}\text{.}\) So the left hand side of the differential equation is also a constant, that depends on \(r\text{,}\) times \(e^{rt}\text{.}\) So we just have to choose \(r\) so that the constant is zero.in-context
