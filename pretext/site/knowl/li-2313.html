Step 1: Since \(|\cos n|\le 1\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \left|\frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right| \le \frac{\sqrt{n}}{n^2-1} \end{equation*}

for all \(n\gt 1\text{.}\) So, by part (a) of the comparison test, which is TheoremÂ 3.3.8, if the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=5}^\infty \frac{\sqrt{n}}{n^2-1}\) converges, then we will have that the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=5}^\infty \left|\frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right|\) also converges, and hence that the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=5}^\infty \frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\) converges absolutely.
