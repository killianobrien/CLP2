Solution 2: Alternately, we could note the following:

  • Since all derivative of \(f(x)\) exist, all its derivatives are continuous.
  • The derivative of an odd function is even, and the derivative of an even function is odd.
  • So, the even-indexed derivatives of \(f(x)\) are continuous, odd functions.
  • Every continuous, odd function passes through the origin. That is, \(f^{(2n)}(0)=0\text{.}\)
  • So, every term in the series is \(0\text{.}\)
