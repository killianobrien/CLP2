The stone at position \(x\) has mass \(\dfrac{1}{4^x}\) kg, and we have to pull it a distance of \(2^x\) metres, so the work involved in moving that one stone is

\begin{equation*} \left(\frac{1}{4^x}\text{ kg}\right)\left(2^x\text{ m}\right)\left(9.8 \frac{\text{m}}{\text{sec}^2}\right) = \frac{9.8}{2^x}\text{ J} \end{equation*}

Therefore, the work to move all the stones is:

\begin{align*} \sum_{x=1}^{\infty}\frac{9.8}{2^x}& =\sum_{x=0}^{\infty}\frac{9.8}{2^{x+1}}\\ &=\sum_{x=0}^{\infty}\frac{9.8}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2^{x}} =\frac{9.8}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{1-\frac12}=9.8 \text{ J} \end{align*}
