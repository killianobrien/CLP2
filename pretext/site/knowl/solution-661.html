Solution 3.5.3.4.1

According to TheoremÂ 3.5.9, because \(f(x)\) diverges somewhere, and because it converges at a point other than its centre, \(f(x)\) has a positive radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\) That is, \(f(x)\) converges whenever \(|x-5| \lt R\text{,}\) and it diverges whenever \(|x-5| \gt R\text{.}\)

If \(R \gt 6\text{,}\) then \(|11-5| \lt R\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\) converges at \(x=11\text{;}\) since we are told \(f(x)\) diverges at \(x=11\text{,}\) we see \(R \le 6\text{.}\)

If \(R \lt 6\text{,}\) then \(|-1-5| \gt R\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\) diverges at \(x=-1\text{;}\) since we are told \(f(x)\) converges at \(x=-1\text{,}\) we see \(R \ge 6\text{.}\)

Therefore, \(R=6\text{.}\)
