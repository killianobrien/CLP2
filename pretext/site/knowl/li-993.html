Step 1. We first check to see if a polynomial \(P(x)\) is needed. To do so, we check to see if the degree of the numerator, \(N(x)\text{,}\) is strictly smaller than the degree of the denominator \(D(x)\text{.}\) In this example, the numerator, \(x-3\text{,}\) has degree one and that is indeed strictly smaller than the degree of the denominator, \(x^2-3x+2\text{,}\) which is two. In this case  3  we do not need to extract a polynomial \(P(x)\) and we move on to step 2.
