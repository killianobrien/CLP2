Consider the integral:

\begin{gather*} \int_0^3 (7 + x^3) \,\,\dee{x} .\qquad\qquad (*) \end{gather*}
  1. Approximate this integral using the left Riemann sum with \(n=3\) intervals.
  2. Write down the expression for the right Riemann sum with \(n\) intervals and calculate the sum. Now take the limit \(n \to \infty\) in your expression for the Riemann sum, to evaluate the integral (\(*\)) exactly.

You may use the identity

\begin{gather*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} i^3 = \frac{n^4 +2n^3 + n^2}{4} \end{gather*}
