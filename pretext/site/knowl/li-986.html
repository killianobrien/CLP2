Item 1.9.2.28.b
Suppose we are evaluating an integral that contains the term \(\sqrt{a^2+x^2}\text{,}\) where \(a\) is a positive constant, and we use the substitution \(x=a\tan u\) (with inverse \(u = \arctan(x/a)\)), so that
\begin{equation*} \sqrt{a^2+x^2} = \sqrt{a^2\sec^2u}= |a\sec u| \end{equation*}
Under what circumstances is \(|a\sec u|\neq a\sec u\text{?}\)
