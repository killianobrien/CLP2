Item 1.12.4.26.b
Since \(f(x)\) is even, using the reasoning of Example 1.2.10,
\begin{align*} \int_{-\infty}^{-1} f(x) \dee{x}\amp=\lim_{t \to \infty}\int_{-t}^{-1}f(x) \dee{x}=\lim_{t \to \infty}\int_{1}^{t} f(x) \dee{x}\\ \amp=\lim_{t \to \infty} \int_{1}^{t} f(x) \dee{x}\\ \end{align*}

Since \(\displaystyle\int_1^\infty f(x) \dee{x}\) converges, the last limit above converges. Since \(f(x)\) is continuous everywhere, by Theorem 1.12.20, \(\displaystyle\int_{-1}^\infty f(x) \dee{x}\) converges (note the adjusted lower limit). Then, since

\begin{align*} \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x) \dee{x}& = \int_{-\infty}^{-1} f(x) \dee{x} + \int_{-1}^\infty f(x) \dee{x} \end{align*}
and both terms converge, our original integral converges as well.
