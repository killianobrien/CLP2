Solution 1.13.27.1

Since \(\tan x =\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int \sin x \cos x \tan x\dee{x}&=\int \sin^2 x\dee{x} = \int \frac{1}{2}\big(1-\cos(2x) \big)\dee{x}\\ &=\frac12\left(x - \frac{1}{2}\sin(2x) \right)+C\\ &=\frac12\left(x -\sin x \cos x \right)+C \end{align*}
