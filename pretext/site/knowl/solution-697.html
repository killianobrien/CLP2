Solution 3.6.8.13.1

We just need to substitute \(y=x^3\) into the known Maclaurin series for \(\sin y\text{,}\) to get the Maclaurin series for \(\sin(x^3)\text{,}\) and then multiply the result by \(x^2\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \sin y&= y-\frac{y^3}{3!}+\cdots \cr \sin(x^3)&= x^3-\frac{x^9}{3!}+\cdots \cr x^2\sin(x^3)&= x^5-\frac{x^{11}}{3!}+\cdots \cr \end{align*}

so \(a=1\) and \(b=-\frac{1}{3!}=-\frac{1}{6}\text{.}\)
