Item c
If the integral \(\int_r^R f(x)\dee{x}\) exists for all \(r \lt R\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x)\dee{x}=\lim_{r\rightarrow-\infty}\int_r^c f(x)\dee{x} +\lim_{R\rightarrow\infty}\int_c^R f(x)\dee{x} \end{equation*}
when both limits exist (and are finite). Any \(c\) can be used.
