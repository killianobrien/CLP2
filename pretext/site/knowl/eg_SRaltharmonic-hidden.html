We have already seen, in Example 3.3.6, that the harmonic series \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n}\) diverges. On the other hand, the series \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^{n-1}\frac{1}{n}\) converges by the alternating series test with \(A_n=\frac{1}{n}\text{.}\) Note that

  1. \(A_n=\frac{1}{n}\ge 0\) for all \(n\ge 1\text{,}\) so that \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty(-1)^{n-1}\frac{1}{n}\) really is an alternating series, and
  2. \(A_n=\frac{1}{n}\) decreases as \(n\) increases, and
  3. \(\lim\limits_{n\rightarrow\infty}A_n =\lim\limits_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1}{n}=0\text{.}\)
