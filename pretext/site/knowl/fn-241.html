Footnote B.2.3
It is obvious that, in the special case that \(z=x\) with \(x\) real, the definitions (EZ) and B.2.1 are equivalent. So to complete the proof of equivalence in the general case \(z=x+iy\text{,}\) it suffices to prove that \(e^{x+iy}=e^x e^{iy}\) under both (EZ) and Definition B.2.1. For Definition B.2.1, this follows from Lemma B.2.2, below.in-context
