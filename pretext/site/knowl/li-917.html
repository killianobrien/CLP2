Set \(x = \sec u\) and \(\dee{x}=\sec u \tan u \dee{u}\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} \int \frac{x^2}{\sqrt{x^2-1}} \dee{x} &= \int \frac{\sec^2 u}{\sqrt{\sec^2u-1}} \sec u \tan u \dee{u}\\ &= \int \sec^3 u \cdot \frac{ \tan u}{\sqrt{\tan^2u}} \dee{u} \qquad \text{since $\tan^2u = \sec^2u-1$}\\ &= \int \sec^3u \cdot \frac{\tan u}{|\tan u|} \dee{u} \end{align*}
