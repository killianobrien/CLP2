We are to find the area of the shaded region. Each point \((x,y)\) in this shaded region has \(-1\le x\le 1\) and \(x \le y \le 4-x^2\text{.}\) When we were defining the integral (way back in DefinitionÂ 1.1.9) we used \(a\) and \(b\) to denote the smallest and largest allowed values of \(x\text{;}\) let's do that here too. Let's also use \(B(x)\) to denote the bottom curve (i.e. to denote the smallest allowed value of \(y\) for a given \(x\)) and use \(T(x)\) to denote the top curve (i.e. to denote the largest allowed value of \(y\) for a given \(x\)). So in this example
\begin{align*} a=-1&& b=1&& B(x)=x&& T(x)=4-x^2 \end{align*}
and the shaded region is
\begin{gather*} \big\{\ (x,y)\ \big|\ a\le x\le b,\ B(x)\le y\le T(x)\ \big\} \end{gather*}
