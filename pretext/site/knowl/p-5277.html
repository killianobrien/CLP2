Now, to confirm that our intuition is correct, we'll first try the limit comparison theorem, which is TheoremÂ 3.3.11, with \(a_n=\left| \frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right|\) and \(b_n = \frac{1}{n^{3/2}}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{a_n}{b_n} &=\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\left|\frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right| }{\frac{1}{n^{3/2}}} = \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{\sqrt{n}\cdot\sqrt{n}^3|\cos n|}{n^2-1}\\ &=\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n^2|\cos n|}{n^2-1} = \lim_{n \to \infty}\left(\frac{1}{1-1/n^2}\right)|\cos n|\\ & = \lim_{n \to \infty}1\cdot|\cos n| \end{align*}

Unfortunately, this limit doesn't exist, so this attempt to use the limit comparison theorem has failed. Fortunately, having seen that the \(\cos n\) caused the failure, it is not hard to adjust our strategy to get a successful proof of absolute convergence.
