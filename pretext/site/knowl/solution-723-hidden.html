We use that

\begin{equation*} \log(1+y)= \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n-1} \frac{y^n}{n} \qquad \text{for all } -1 \lt y \le 1 \end{equation*}

with \(y=\dfrac{x-2}{2}\) to give

\begin{align*} \log(x) & = \log(2 +x -2) =\log\left[2\left(1+\frac{x-2}{2}\right)\right]\\ &= \log 2 +\log\Big(1+\frac{x-2}{2}\Big) =\log 2 +\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n\,2^n}(x-2)^n \end{align*}

It converges when \(-1 \lt y\le 1\text{,}\) or equivalently, \(0 \lt x\le 4\text{.}\)
