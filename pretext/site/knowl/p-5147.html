We apply the limit comparison test with \(b_k=\frac{1}{k^{5/3}}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{gather*} \lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{a_k}{b_k} =\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{\root{3}\of{k}/(k^2-k)}{1/k^{5/3}} =\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{k^2}{k^2-k} =\lim_{k\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1}{1-1/k} =1 \end{gather*}

exists and is nonzero, and \(\sum\limits_{k=1}^\infty\frac{1}{k^{5/3}}\) converges (by the \(p\)-test with \(p=\frac{5}{3} \gt 1\)), the given series converges by the limit comparison test.
