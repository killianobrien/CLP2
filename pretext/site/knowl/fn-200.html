Footnote 3.6.8
Recall that when we solve of a separable differential equation our general solution will have an arbitrary constant in it. That constant cannot be determined from the differential equation alone and we need some extra data to find it. This extra information is often information about the system at its beginning (for example when position or time is zero) — hence “initial conditions”. Of course the reader is already familiar with this because it was covered back in Section 2.4.in-context
