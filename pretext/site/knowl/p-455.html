Let us compute \(\int_{-1}^1 \big(1-|x|\big)\dee{x}\) again. In Example 1.1.15 we evaluated this integral by interpreting it as the area of a triangle. This time we are going to use only the properties given in Theorems 1.2.1 and 1.2.3 and the facts that

\begin{align*} \int_a^b \dee{x} &= b-a &\text{and}&& \int_a^b x\dee{x}=\frac{b^2-a^2}{2} \end{align*}

That \(\int_a^b\dee{x} = b-a\) is part (e) of Theorem 1.2.1. We saw that \(\int_a^b x\dee{x}=\frac{b^2-a^2}{2}\) in Example 1.2.6.
