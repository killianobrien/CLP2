The question remaining is what do to with the last series. If we write out the terms, we see the difference between \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n\) and \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty c_{n+1}\) is simply that the latter is missing \(c_1\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_{n+1}&=c_2+c_3+c_4+c_5+\cdots\\ &=\textcolor{red}{-c_1+c_1}+c_2+c_3+c_4+c_5+\cdots\\ &=\textcolor{red}{-c_1}+\sum_{n=1}^\infty c_{n}\\ \end{align*}

So,

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty\left( a_n+b_n+c_{n+1}\right)&=A+B+C-\textcolor{red}{c_1} \end{align*}
