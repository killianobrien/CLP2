The complex plane is simply the \(xy\)-plane equipped with an addition operation and a multiplication operation. A complex number is nothing more than a point in that \(xy\)-plane. It is conventional to use the notation \(x+iy\) 1  to stand for the complex number \((x,y)\text{.}\) In other words, it is conventional to write \(x\) in place of \((x,0)\) and \(i\) in place of \((0,1)\text{.}\)
