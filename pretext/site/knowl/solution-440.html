Solution 2.2.2.12.1

By definition, the average value is

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{e-1}\int_1^e\frac{\log x}{x}\dee{x}&\\ \end{align*}

To integrate, we use the substitution \(u=\log x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}\text{.}\) Then the limits of integration become 0 and 1, respectively.

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{e-1}\int_1^e\frac{\log x}{x}\dee{x}& =\frac{1}{e-1}\int_0^1u\dee{u} =\frac{1}{e-1}\bigg[\frac{u^2}{2}\bigg]_0^1 =\frac{1}{2(e-1)} \end{align*}
