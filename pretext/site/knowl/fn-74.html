Footnote 1.10.8
To be precise, the quadratic equation \(ax^2+bx+c=0\) has solutions \(x = \frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}.\) The term \(b^2-4ac\) is called the discriminant and it tells us about the number of solutions. If the discriminant is positive then there are two real solutions. When it is zero, there is a single solution. And if it is negative, there is no real solutions (you need complex numbers to say more than this).in-context
