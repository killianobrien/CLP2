Now we can perform our partial fraction decomposition.
\begin{align*} \frac{x^2+2x-1}{x^4-2x^3+x^2}&= \frac{x^2+2x-1}{x^2(x-1)^2} = \frac{A}{x}+\frac{B}{x^2}+\frac{C}{x-1}+\frac{D}{(x-1)^2}\\ \end{align*}

Multiply both sides by the original denominator.

\begin{align*} x^2+2x-1&=Ax(x\!-\!1)^2+B(x\!-\!1)^2+Cx^2(x\!-\!1)+Dx^2\tag{1}\\ \end{align*}

To be sneaky, we set \(x=0\text{,}\) and find:

\begin{align*} \color{red}{-1}&\color{red}{=B}\\ \end{align*}

We also set \(x=1\text{,}\) and find:

\begin{align*} \color{red}{2}&\color{red}{=D}\\ \end{align*}

We use \(B\) and \(D\) to simplify Equation (1).

\begin{align*} x^2+2x-1&=Ax(x-1)^2\textcolor{red}{-1}(x-1)^2+Cx^2(x-1)+\textcolor{red}{2}x^2\\ 0&=Ax(x-1)^2+Cx^2(x-1)\\ &=x(x-1)[(A+C)x-A]\\ \mbox{So,}\qquad 0&=(A+C)x-A \end{align*}
That is, \(\textcolor{red}{A=C=0}\text{.}\)
