  1. The rate of change of speed at time \(t\) is \(-kv(t)^2\) for some constant of proportionality \(k\) (to be determined--but we assume it is positive, since the speed is decreasing). So \(v(t)\) obeys the differential equation \(\diff{v}{t}=-kv^2\) .
  2. The equation \(\diff{v}{t}=-kv^2\) is a separable differential equation, which we can solve in the usual way.
    \begin{align*} \diff{v}{t}&=-kv^2\\ \frac{\dee{v}}{-v^2}&=k\dee{t}\\ \int -\frac{\dee{v}}{v^2} &=\int k\dee{t}\\ \frac{1}{v}&=kt+C\\ \end{align*}

    At time \(t=0\text{,}\) \(v=400\text{,}\) so \(C=\frac{1}{400}\text{.}\) Then:

    \begin{align*} \frac{1}{v}&=kt+\frac{1}{400}\tag{$*$}\\ \end{align*}

    At time \(t=1\text{,}\) \(v=200\text{,}\) so

    \begin{align*} \frac{1}{200}&=k+\frac{1}{400}\\ k&=\frac{1}{400}\\ \end{align*}

    Therefore, from (\(*\)),

    \begin{align*} \frac{1}{v}&=\frac{t}{400}+\frac{1}{400} = \frac{t+1}{400}\\ v&=\frac{400}{t+1} \end{align*}
  3. To find when the speed is 50, we set \(v=50\) in the equation from (b) and solve for \(t\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} 50&=\frac{400}{t+1}\\ 50(t+1)&=400\\ t+1&=8\\ t&=7 \end{align*}
