By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 1,

\begin{alignat*}{2} F'(x)&=\diff{}{x} \int_0^x \log(2+\sin t)\,\dee{t} &&=\log(2+\sin x)\\ G'(y)&=\diff{}{y}\bigg[- \int_0^y \log(2+\sin t)\,\dee{t}\bigg] &&=-\log(2+\sin y) \end{alignat*}

So,

\begin{equation*} F'\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}\Big)=\log 3\qquad G'\Big(\frac{\pi}{2}\Big)=-\log(3) \end{equation*}
