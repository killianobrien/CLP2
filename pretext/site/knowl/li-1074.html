Now set \(y=(x+2)/3, \dee{y}=\frac{1}{3}\dee{x}\text{,}\) or equivalently \(x=3y-2, \dee{x}=3\dee{y}\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \int \frac{2x+7}{x^2+4x+13}\dee{x} &= \int \frac{2x+7}{(x+2)^2+3^2}\dee{x}\\ &= \int \frac{6y-4+7}{3^2 y^2 + 3^2} \cdot 3 \dee{y}\\ &= \int \frac{6y+3}{3(y^2+1)}\dee{y}\\ &= \int \frac{2y+1}{y^2+1}\dee{y} \end{align*}
Notice that we chose 3 in \(y=(x+2)/3\) precisely to transform the denominator into the form \(y^2+1\text{.}\)
