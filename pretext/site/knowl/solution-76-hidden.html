1. We differentiate with respect to \(a\text{.}\) Recall \(\diff{}{x}\{\arccos x\} = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\text{.}\) To differentiate \(\frac{1}{2}a\sqrt{1-a^2}\text{,}\) we use the product and chain rules.
    \begin{align*} &\diff{}{a}\left\{\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2}\arccos(a)+\frac{1}{2}a\sqrt{1-a^2}\right\}\\ &=0-\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-a^2}} + \left(\frac{1}{2}a\right)\cdot\frac{-2a}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}} + \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1-a^2}\\ &=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}}- \frac{a^2}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}}+\frac{1-a^2}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}}\\ &=\frac{1-a^2+1-a^2}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}}\\ &=\frac{2(1-a^2)}{2\sqrt{1-a^2}}\\ &=\sqrt{1-a^2} \end{align*}
