To express \(\sin x\) and \(\cos x\) in terms of \(u\text{,}\) we first use the double angle trig identities (Equations 1.8.2 and 1.8.3 with \(x \mapsto \frac{x}{2}\)) to express \(\sin x\) and \(\cos x\) in terms of \(\sin\frac{x}{2}\) and \(\cos\frac{x}{2}\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \sin x &= 2 \sin\frac{x}{2} \cos\frac{x}{2}\\ \cos x &= \cos^2 \frac{x}{2} - \sin^2\frac{x}{2} \end{align*}
