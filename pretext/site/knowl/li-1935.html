Item 2.4.7.28.b
The equation \(\diff{v}{t}=-kv^2\) is a separable differential equation, which we can solve in the usual way.
\begin{align*} \diff{v}{t}&=-kv^2\\ \frac{\dee{v}}{-v^2}&=k\dee{t}\\ \int -\frac{\dee{v}}{v^2} &=\int k\dee{t}\\ \frac{1}{v}&=kt+C\\ \end{align*}

At time \(t=0\text{,}\) \(v=400\text{,}\) so \(C=\frac{1}{400}\text{.}\) Then:

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{v}&=kt+\frac{1}{400}\tag{$*$}\\ \end{align*}

At time \(t=1\text{,}\) \(v=200\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{200}&=k+\frac{1}{400}\\ k&=\frac{1}{400}\\ \end{align*}

Therefore, from (\(*\)),

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{v}&=\frac{t}{400}+\frac{1}{400} = \frac{t+1}{400}\\ v&=\frac{400}{t+1} \end{align*}
