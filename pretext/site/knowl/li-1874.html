Item 2.4.7.1.b
If \(y=\dfrac{-2}{x^2+1}\text{,}\) then \(\diff{y}{x} = \dfrac{4x}{(x+1)^2}\text{.}\) Let's see whether this is equal to \(xy^2\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} xy^2&=x\left(\frac{-2}{x^2+1}\right)^2\\ &=\frac{4x}{(x^2+1)^2}\\ &=\diff{y}{x} \end{align*}
So, \(y=\dfrac{-2}{x^2+1}\) is indeed a solution to the differential equation \(\diff{y}{x}=yx^2\text{.}\)
