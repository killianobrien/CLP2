We perform integration by parts with \(u=\log x\) and \(\dee{v}=x^{-7}\,\dee{x}\text{,}\) so that \(\dee{u}=\frac{\dee{x}}{x}\) and \(v = -\frac{x^{-6}}{6}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \int \frac{\log x}{x^7}\,\dee{x} &=\underbrace{ -\log x\ \frac{x^{-6}}{6}}_{uv} + \int \underbrace{\frac{x^{-6}}{6}}_{-v}\ \underbrace{\frac{\dee{x}}{x}}_{\dee{u}} = - \frac{\log x}{6 x^6} +\frac{1}{6} \int x^{-7} \dee{x}\\ &= - \frac{\log x}{6 x^6} - \frac{1}{36 x^6} + C \end{align*}
