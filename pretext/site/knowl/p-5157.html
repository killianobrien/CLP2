By the alternating series test, the error introduced when we approximate the series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{(-1)^n}{n\cdot 10^n}\) by \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^N\frac{(-1)^n}{n\cdot 10^n}\) is at most the magnitude of the first omitted term, \(\displaystyle\frac1{(N+1) 10^{(N+1)}}\text{.}\) By trial and error, we find that this expression becomes smaller than \(10^{-6}\) when \(N+1\ge 6\text{.}\) So the smallest allowable value is \(N=5\text{.}\)
