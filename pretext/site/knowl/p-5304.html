\begin{align*} -\frac14&\le \frac{\sin n}{4} \le \frac14\\ \Rightarrow \qquad\left(-\frac{1}{4}-\frac{1}{8}\right)&\le\left( \frac{\sin x}{4} -\frac18\right) \lt \left(\frac14-\frac18\right)\\ \Rightarrow \qquad -\frac{3}{8} &\le\left( \frac{\sin x}{4} -\frac18\right) \lt \frac{1}{8}\\ \Rightarrow \qquad 0 &\le\left| \frac{\sin x}{4} -\frac18\right| \lt \frac{3}{8}\\ \Rightarrow \qquad 0 &\le\left|\left( \frac{\sin x}{4} -\frac18\right)^n\right| \lt \left(\frac{3}{8}\right)^n \end{align*}

Since \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{3}{8}\right)^n\) converges (it's a geometric sum with \(|r| \lt 1\)), by the direct comparison test, \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left|\left( \frac{\sin x}{4} -\frac18\right)^n\right|\) converges as well.
