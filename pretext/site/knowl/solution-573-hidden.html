We recognize this as a telescoping series.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c||cc||r} \mathbf{k}&\mathbf{\frac{6}{k^2}}&\mathbf{-\frac{6}{(k+1)^2}}&\mathbf{s_k}\\ \hline 1&6&\color{red}{-\frac{6}{4}}&6-\color{red}{\frac64}\\ 2&\color{red}{\frac{6}{4}}&\color{orange}{-\frac{6}{9}}&6-\color{orange}{\frac69}\\ 3&\color{orange}{\frac{6}{9}}&\color{green}{-\frac{6}{16}}&6-\color{green}{\frac{6}{16}}\\ 4&\color{green}{\frac{6}{16}}&\color{blue}{-\frac{6}{25}}&6-\color{blue}{\frac{6}{25}}\\ 5&\color{blue}{\frac{6}{25}}&\color{purple}{-\frac{6}{36}}&6-\color{purple}{\frac{6}{36}}\\ 6&\color{purple}{\frac{6}{36}}&-\frac{6}{47}&6-\frac{6}{47}\\ \vdots&&& \end{array} \end{equation*}

When we compute the \(n^{\rm th}\) partial sum, i.e. the sum of of the first \(n\) terms, successive terms cancel and only the first half of the first term, \(\Big( \frac{6}{k^2} - \frac{6}{(k+1)^2} \Big)\Big|_{k=1}\text{,}\) and the second half of the \(n^{\rm th}\) term, \(\Big( \frac{6}{k^2} - \frac{6}{(k+1)^2} \Big)\Big|_{k=n}\text{,}\) survive. That is:

\begin{gather*} s_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \bigg( \frac{6}{k^2} - \frac{6}{(k+1)^2} \bigg) = \frac6{1^2} - \frac{6}{(n+1)^2} \end{gather*}

Therefore, we can see directly that the sequence of partial sums \(\{ s_n \}\) is convergent:

\begin{gather*} \lim_{n\to\infty} s_n = \lim_{n\to\infty} \bigg( 6- \frac{6}{(n+1)^2} \bigg) = 6 \end{gather*}
