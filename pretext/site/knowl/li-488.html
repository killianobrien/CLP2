The straightforward method: We use the substitution \(\textcolor{red}{u=x^2}\text{,}\) for which \(\textcolor{blue}{\dee{u}=2x\,\dee{x}}\text{,}\) and note that \(u=4\) for both \(x=2\) and \(x=-2\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \int_{-2}^2 xe^{x^2}\,\dee{x} =\int_{-2}^2 \frac{1}{2}e^{\textcolor{red}{x^2}}\,\textcolor{blue}{2x\dee{x}} = \int_4^4 \frac{1}{2}e^{\textcolor{red}{u}}\,\textcolor{blue}{\dee{u}} =0 \end{equation*}
