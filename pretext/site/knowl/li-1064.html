We'll start by converting it into the integral of a rational function using the substitution \(u=\sin x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=\cos x\dee{x}\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \int \sec^3 x\dee{x} &=\int \frac{1}{\cos^3 x}\dee{x} & \text{massage this a little}\\ &=\int \frac{\cos x}{\cos^4 x}\dee{x} &\text{replace $\cos^2x=1-\sin^2x=1-u^2$}\\ &=\int \frac{\cos x\dee{x}}{{[1-\sin^2 x]}^2}\\ &=\int \frac{\dee{u}}{ [1-u^2]^2} \end{align*}
