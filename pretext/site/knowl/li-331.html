Item 1.2.3.16.b
The values of \(x\) in the domain of the function above are those that satisfy \(1-(ax)^2 \geq 0\text{.}\) That is, \(-\frac{1}{a}\leq x \leq \frac{1}{a}\text{.}\) Therefore, the upper half of the ellipse has area
\begin{align*} \displaystyle\frac{1}{b}&\displaystyle\int_{-\frac{1}{a}}^{\frac{1}{a}}\sqrt{1-(ax)^2}\dee{x}\\ \end{align*}

The upper half of a circle has equation \(y=\sqrt{r^2-x^2}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} &=\frac{1}{b}\int_{-\frac{1}{a}}^{\frac{1}{a}}\sqrt{a^2\left(\frac{1}{a^2}-x^2\right)}\dee{x}\\ &=\frac{1}{b}\int_{-\frac{1}{a}}^{\frac{1}{a}}a\sqrt{\frac{1}{a^2}-x^2}\dee{x}\\ &=\frac{a}{b}\int_{-\frac{1}{a}}^{\frac{1}{a}}\sqrt{\frac{1}{a^2}-x^2}\dee{x} \end{align*}
