Item 1.10.4.4.a

We start by dividing. The leading term of the numerator is \(x\) times the leading term of the denominator. The remainder is \(x+2\text{.}\)

That is, \(x^3+2x+2 = x(x^2+1)+(x+2)\text{.}\) So,

\begin{equation*} \frac{x^3+2x+2}{x^2+1} = x+\frac{x+2}{x^2+1} \end{equation*}
