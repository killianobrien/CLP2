(d) We know the derivative of arctangent, and it would integrate nicely if multiplied to the antiderivative of \(x\text{.}\) So, we integrate by parts with \(u=\arctan x\) and \(\dee{v}=x\,\dee{x}\) so that \(\dee{u}=\frac{1}{1+x^2}\dee{x}\) and \(v=\frac{1}{2} x^2\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \int x\arctan x\,\dee{x}&= \frac{1}{2} x^2\arctan x -\frac{1}{2}\int \frac{x^2}{1+x^2}\,\dee{x}\\ &= \frac{1}{2} x^2\arctan x-\frac{1}{2}\int \frac{1+x^2-1}{1+x^2}\,\dee{x}\cr &= \frac{1}{2} x^2\arctan x-\frac{1}{2}\int \left(1-\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right)\,\dee{x}\cr &=\frac{1}{2}\big[ x^2\arctan x -x +\arctan x\big]+C \end{align*}
