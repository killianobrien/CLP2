(a) As in Question 2, since

\begin{align*} s_{n-1}& = a_1+a_2+ \cdots + a_{n-1}\\ s_{n}& = a_1+a_2+ \cdots + a_{n-1} +a_n \end{align*}

we can find \(a_n\) by subtracting:

\begin{align*} a_n &= s_n - s_{n-1}\\ &=\frac{1 + 3n}{5+4n} - \frac{1 + 3(n-1)}{5+4(n-1)} = \frac{3n+1}{4n+5} - \frac{3n - 2}{4n+1}\\ &= \frac{(3n+1)(4n+1) - (3n - 2) (4n+5)}{(4n+1)(4n+5)}\\ &= \frac{11}{16n^2 + 24n +5} \end{align*}

(b) Using Definition 3.2.3,

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n&= \lim_{n \to \infty} s_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1 + 3n}{5+4n} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1/n + 3}{5/n+4} = \frac{0+3}{0+4} = \frac{3}{4} \end{align*}

The series converges to \(\dfrac{3}{4}\text{.}\)
