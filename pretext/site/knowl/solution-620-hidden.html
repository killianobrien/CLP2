We first develop some intuition. For very large \(n\text{,}\) \(3n^2\) dominates \(7\) so that

\begin{gather*} \frac{\sqrt{3 n^2 - 7}}{n^3} \approx \frac{\sqrt{3 n^2}}{n^3} =\frac{\sqrt{3}}{n^2} \end{gather*}

The series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2}\) converges by the \(p\)-test with \(p=2\text{,}\) so we expect the given series to converge too.

To verify that our intuition is correct, it suffices to observe that

\begin{gather*} 0 \lt a_n = \frac{\sqrt{3 n^2 - 7}}{n^3} \lt \frac{\sqrt{3n^2}}{n^3} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{n^2} =c_n \end{gather*}

for all \(n\ge 2\text{.}\) As the series \(\sum\limits_{n=2}^\infty c_n\) converges, the comparison test says that \(\sum\limits_{n=2}^\infty a_n\) converges too.
