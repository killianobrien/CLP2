The following sketch contains the graphs of \(y=2^x\) and \(y=\sqrt{x}+1\text{.}\)

From the sketch, it looks like the two curves cross when \(x=0\) and when \(x=1\) and nowhere 3  else. Indeed, when \(x=0\) we have \(2^x=\sqrt{x}+1=1\) and when \(x=1\) we have \(2^x=\sqrt{x}+1=2\text{.}\)

To antidifferentiate \(2^x\text{,}\) we write \(2^x={(e^{\log 2})}^x=e^{x\log 2}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \text{Area} &= \int_0^1\big[(\sqrt{x}+1)-e^{x\log 2}\big]\,\dee{x} =\left[\frac{2}{3}x^{3/2}+x-\frac{1}{\log 2} 2^x\right]_0^1\\ &=\frac{2}{3}+1-\frac{1}{\log 2}[2-1] =\frac{5}{3}-\frac{1}{\log 2} \end{align*}
