Just as we did in the previous example, we multiply this equation by a constant to remove this unwanted factor:
\begin{align*} (-1)\cdot \diff{}{x}\cos x &= (-1)\cdot(-\sin x) &\text{giving us}\\ \diff{}{x}\big(-\cos x\big) &= \sin x \end{align*}
This tells us that \(-\cos x\) is an antiderivative of \(\sin x\text{.}\)
