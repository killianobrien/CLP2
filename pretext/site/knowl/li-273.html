The definite integral in (e), \(\int_a^b 1 \dee{x}\text{,}\) can be interpreted geometrically as the area of the rectangle with height 1 running from \(x=a\) to \(x=b\text{;}\) this area is clearly \(b-a\text{.}\) We can also prove this formula from the definition of the integral (DefinitionÂ 1.1.9):
\begin{align*} \int_a^b\dee{x} &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n f(x_{i,n}^*)\,\frac{b-a}{n} &\text{by definition}\\ &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n 1\,\frac{b-a}{n} &\text{since $f(x)=1$}\\ &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}(b-a) \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{1}{n} &\text{since $a,b$ are constants}\\ &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}(b-a)\\ &=b-a \end{align*}
as required.
