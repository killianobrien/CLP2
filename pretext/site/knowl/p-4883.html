(a) By hypothesis \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n\) converges. So it suffices to prove that \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty [Kc_n-a_n]\) converges, because then, by our Arithmetic of series Theorem 3.2.9,

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n = \sum_{n=0}^\infty K c_n -\sum_{n=0}^\infty [Kc_n-a_n] \end{equation*}

will converge too. But for all \(n\ge N_0\text{,}\) \(Kc_n-a_n\ge 0\) so that, for all \(N\ge N_0\text{,}\) the partial sums

\begin{equation*} S_N = \sum_{n=0}^N [Kc_n-a_n] \end{equation*}

increase with \(N\text{,}\) but never gets bigger than the finite number \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^{N_0} [Kc_n-a_n] + K \sum\limits_{n=N_0+1}^\infty c_n\text{.}\) So the partial sums \(S_N\) converge as \(N\rightarrow\infty\text{.}\)
