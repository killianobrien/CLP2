In this optional section we give a more mathematically rigorous definition of the definite integral \(\ds \int_a^b f(x)\dee{x}\text{.}\) Some textbooks use a sneakier, but equivalent, definition. The integral will be defined as the limit of a family of approximations to the area between the graph of \(y=f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis, with \(x\) running from \(a\) to \(b\text{.}\) We will then show conditions under which this limit is guaranteed to exist.
