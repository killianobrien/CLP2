Item e
The ODE (B.2.1) is said to have constant coefficients if the coefficients \(a_0(t)\text{,}\) \(a_1(t)\text{,}\) \(\cdots\text{,}\) \(a_n(t)\) are all constants. Otherwise, it is said to have variable coefficients. For example, the ODE \(y''(t)+7y(t)=\sin t\) is constant coefficient, while \(y''(t)+ty(t)=\sin t\) is variable coefficient.
