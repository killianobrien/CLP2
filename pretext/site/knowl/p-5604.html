  1. If \(G(t)\) is differentiable 11  and \(G'(c)\) is nonzero for all \(c\) strictly between \(a\) and \(x\text{,}\) then the Taylor remainder
    \begin{equation*} E_n(a,x)=\frac{1}{n!} f^{(n+1)}(c)\,\frac{G(x)-G(a)}{G'(c)}\, (x-c)^n \end{equation*}
    for some \(c\) strictly between \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\)
  2. (Cauchy form)
    \begin{equation*} E_n(a,x)=\frac{1}{n!}f^{(n+1)}(c)\, (x-c)^n(x-a) \end{equation*}
    for some \(c\) strictly between \(a\) and \(x\text{.}\)
