As we saw in in ExampleÂ 3.5.21,

\begin{equation*} \arctan x = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \end{equation*}

which is an alternating series when \(x\) is nonzero. If we use its partial sum \(S_N\) to approximate \(\arctan x\text{,}\) the absolute error involved is no more than

\begin{equation*} \frac{|x|^{2(N+1)+1}}{N(n+1)+1} =\frac{|x|^{2N+3}}{2N+3} \end{equation*}

We want this error to be at most \(10^{-6}\) whenever \(-\frac14 \lt x \lt \frac14\text{.}\) For this range of \(x\) values, \(\dfrac{|x|^{2N+3}}{2N+3} \lt \dfrac{1}{(2N+3)4^{2N+3}}\text{,}\) so we want \(N\) that satisfies the inequality:

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(2N+3)4^{2N+3}}\le \frac{1}{10^5} \end{equation*}

A quick check with a calculator shows that \(N=2\) suffices.

So, the partial sum

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^2 (-1)^n\frac{x^{2n+1}}{2n+1}=x-\frac{x^3}{3}+\frac{x^5}{5} \end{equation*}

approximates \(\arctan x\) to within an error of \(\dfrac{x^7}{7}\text{.}\)

When \(x\) is between \(-\frac14\) and \(\frac14\text{,}\) that error is at most \(\dfrac{1}{7\cdot 4^7}=\dfrac{1}{114688} \lt \dfrac{1}{100000}=10^{-5}\text{,}\) as desired. (When \(x=0\text{,}\) our approximation is 0, the exact value of \(\arctan 0\text{.}\))
